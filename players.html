Some of the Involved

SNCC

The Student Nonviolence Coordinating Comittee was run by many whites (such as Bob Zellner) and blacks for the sole purpose of getting the right education for the ones who can't provide their cities with. They launched "Freedom Schools" in the South to allow Blacks to get the education they deserved.

Juliette Hampton Morgan

Morgan, a librarian, didn't have a car in her city of Montgomery, so she had to ride the bus. Seeing the treatment of blacks on the bus, she took action by writing letters to The Montgomery Advertiser about the mistreatment she sees. Hatred by many surrounding passengers, co-workers, and bus drivers led to a cross being burnt on her lawn and the death of her by intentional drug overdose due to death threats.

Rev. James Reeb

Reeb, a Unitarian minister, came to Selma, Alabama in 1965 to help support voting rights for African American citizens. Three white men beaten him and two other ministers to death, leading to the death of him a couple days after.

Viola Gregg Liuzzo

Liuzzo, found to be the only white woman that died in the Selma to Montgomery leading protesters, was a mother of five and led protesters into the city by car. On the way, driving with a black young man, another car pulled up beside her's and shot her to death. The passenger stayed alive by playing dead, but her death led eventually to an investigation into the Ku Klux Klan.

Jim Letherer

Letherer (in the center with crutches), an amputee who lost his right leg to cancer, found the spirit and need to march to Selma in 1965. He led with Martin Luther King Jr. and kept protesters' spirits high by chanting "Left, left, left!"

William Lewis Moore

Moore, a postman, had planned to send a letter to the Governor of Mississippi. While going on a route to Jackson, Mississippi from Chattanooga, Tennessee for a solo freedom march, he was fatally shot in the head.
